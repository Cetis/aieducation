Speculative Maps to explore Large Language Models in Education

This project is to be used a prompt or speculative tool to help stakeholders consider how to measure, visualise and compare pillars and indicators that reflect the various impacts of Large Language Models on Education. It is informed by emerging signals from media and educators, articles and research on LLM.

Pillars and Indicators

Here is context for pillars and indicators that can help act as speculation for data gathering and evaluation frameworks for Large Language Models (LLM) in education.

Educational Readiness

This pillar identifies a number of indicators that speak to the readiness of education providers regarding the utilisation and responses to LLMs in a country. This also speaks to the experiences of educators.

  1. Educator acceptance
  2. Educator resistance
  3. Technological best practices
  4. Abuse detection

Learner Experience

What are the experiences of learners?

  1. Primary Education experience
  2. Secondary Education experience
  3. Further Education experience
  4. Higher Education experience
  5. Professional learning experience

Policy Readiness

How have government, regulators and public policy responded to the proliferation of LLM?

  1. Technological regulation
  2. Public engagement
  3. Government policy maturity

Technology Readiness

This pillar speaks to how LLMs are being deployed and utilised across digital technologies in a region.

  1. LLM integration
  2. Best practices
  3. Inclusive and accessible technology

Maps

The maps use a filter to show the aggregate score from a particular pillar. The filter is used to select a pillar to visualise. The map also acts as a filter - click or tap a country and it will be selected in the other charts.
